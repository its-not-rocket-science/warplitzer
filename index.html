Warplitzer

Multi-transfer vinyl restoration: combining repeated or differently captured record transfers to investigate cleaner, more reliable audio recovery.

Status: early research prototype

What is Warplitzer?

Warplitzer explores a simple idea: every playback of a vinyl record is a slightly different observation of the same underlying signal.

A click may appear in one transfer and not another. A warp may distort one pass differently from the next. A stylus, cartridge, or playback speed may reveal different information about the same groove.

Warplitzer treats multiple transfers as independent observations, then investigates how they can be aligned and combined to separate shared musical content from pass-specific defects.

What it aims to improve

  • Clicks, pops, and transient defects.
  • Crackle and surface noise.
  • Wow, flutter, and speed variation.
  • Warp-induced timing errors.
  • Tracking variation between different playback passes.

Technical direction

The project is expected to combine FFT-based analysis, cross-correlation, dynamic time warping, phase-aware alignment, robust averaging, outlier rejection, and optional machine-learning approaches.

It is not a finished restoration application yet. It is a research prototype for testing whether multi-transfer reconstruction can recover better audio than a single pass alone.
